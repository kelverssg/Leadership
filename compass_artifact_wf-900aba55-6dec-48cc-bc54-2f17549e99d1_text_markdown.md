# McKinsey's 2025 Reading Guide: 90+ Books That Leaders Recommend

McKinsey's 2025 annual book recommendations showcase an extraordinary collection of **over 90 titles** from 47 global contributors, ranging from CEOs like Bob Sternfels to university presidents and media leaders. **The overarching theme reflects a shared hunger for meaning, progress, and human connection**, with many leaders gravitating toward books that tackle democracy, power, and the ethical implications of AI. This curated list spans ten genres and offers both timely insights and timeless wisdom for navigating today's complex landscape.

The recommendations reveal **three key intellectual currents**: leaders seeking historical perspective on current crises, exploring the intersection of technology and humanity, and pursuing personal transformation through diverse narratives. Notable patterns include strong representation of translated literature, emphasis on leadership psychology, and attention to marginalized voices telling essential stories.

## Leadership and Management Excellence

**A CEO for All Seasons: Mastering the Cycles of Leadership** by Carolyn Dewar, Kurt Strovink, Scott Keller, and Vikram Malhotra (Scribner)
*Recommended by: Raju Narisetti, McKinsey Global Publishing Leader*

This compact guide dissects the four distinct phases every CEO must navigate toward leadership mastery. Building on the authors' bestselling "CEO Excellence," it offers practical frameworks for managing the psychological and emotional demands of executive leadership. **Essential for current and aspiring executives** who need actionable insights on transitioning between leadership phases, from crisis management to transformation leadership.

**Leading Through Disruption: A Changemaker's Guide to Twenty-First Century Leadership** by Andrew N. Liveris
*Recommended by: Zubaida Bai, CEO, Grameen Foundation*

Former Dow Chemical CEO Liveris presents a blueprint for leading through unprecedented change. The book emphasizes adaptive leadership, stakeholder capitalism, and sustainable business practices. **Valuable for executives navigating digital transformation and social responsibility demands**—particularly those in traditional industries facing disruption.

**Red Helicopter—a Parable for Our Times: Lead Change with Kindness (Plus a Little Math)** by James Rhee
*Recommended by: Cristina Mancini, CEO, Black Girls Code*

Rhee's unconventional leadership philosophy combines mathematical thinking with human-centered values. Drawing from his experience transforming Ashley Stewart, he demonstrates how kindness can be a business strategy. **Perfect for leaders seeking alternatives to traditional command-and-control management**, especially those in purpose-driven organizations.

## Historical Perspectives on Power and Democracy

**The Demon of Unrest: A Saga of Hubris, Heartbreak, and Heroism at the Dawn of the Civil War** by Erik Larson (Crown)
*Recommended by: Bob Sternfels, McKinsey Global Managing Partner*

Larson masterfully chronicles the five pivotal months between Lincoln's election and Fort Sumter's bombardment. His narrative illuminates how political hubris and miscommunication can spiral into crisis. **Essential reading for leaders concerned about political polarization and democratic fragility**, offering sobering lessons about the consequences of failed dialogue and compromise.

**The Power Broker: Robert Moses and the Fall of New York** by Robert A. Caro (Knopf)
*Recommended by: McKinsey Global Publishing team*

This Pulitzer Prize-winning biography celebrates its 50th anniversary by examining how one man shaped modern New York through decades of unaccountable power. Caro's meticulous research reveals the corruption inherent in unchecked authority. **Indispensable for anyone interested in power dynamics, urban planning, or public policy**—particularly relevant as cities grapple with infrastructure and governance challenges.

**Book and Dagger: How Scholars and Librarians Became the Unlikely Spies of World War II** by Elyse Graham (Ecco)
*Recommended by: Christopher Eisgruber, Princeton University President*

Graham reveals how academics transformed into intelligence operatives, using research skills to support Allied victory. The book demonstrates how intellectual curiosity and methodical thinking can serve national security. **Fascinating for academics, intelligence professionals, and anyone interested in the intersection of scholarship and practical application**.

## Technology, Psychology, and Modern Life

**The Anxious Generation: How the Great Rewiring of Childhood Is Causing an Epidemic of Mental Illness** by Jonathan Haidt (Penguin Press)
*Recommended by: Cristina Junqueira, Nubank Co-founder*

Haidt examines how smartphone culture has fundamentally altered childhood development, linking screen time to rising anxiety and depression. His research-backed analysis offers practical solutions for parents, educators, and policymakers. **Crucial for parents, educators, and anyone working with young people**—especially valuable for tech leaders grappling with their platforms' societal impact.

**The Unaccountability Machine: Why Big Systems Make Terrible Decisions—and How the World Lost Its Mind** by Dan Davies
*Recommended by: Paul Hudson, Sanofi CEO*

Davies explores why large organizations consistently make poor decisions despite good intentions. He examines systemic failures across institutions from banks to governments. **Essential for executives in large organizations** who need to understand and combat institutional dysfunction, bureaucratic drift, and decision-making pathologies.

**Growing Up in Public: Coming of Age in a Digital World** by Devorah Heitner (Tarcher)
*Recommended by: McKinsey Global Publishing team*

A practical guide for navigating digital parenting challenges, offering frameworks for discussing online presence with children. Heitner provides tools for helping young people develop healthy relationships with technology. **Invaluable for parents and educators** struggling to guide children through digital citizenship and online safety.

## Health, Longevity, and Human Potential

**Why We Sleep: Unlocking the Power of Sleep and Dreams** by Matthew Walker (Scribner)
*Recommended by: Alan Schnitzer, Travelers CEO*

Neuroscientist Walker reveals sleep's fundamental role in physical health, mental performance, and longevity. His research demonstrates how sleep affects everything from creativity to immune function. **Essential for high-performers and anyone seeking to optimize their health**—particularly relevant for executives managing demanding schedules.

**Ordinary Magic: The Science of How We Can Achieve Big Change with Small Acts** by Gregory M. Walton
*Recommended by: Tracy Francis, McKinsey Senior Partner*

Stanford psychologist Walton examines how minor interventions can create significant behavioral and social changes. The book provides evidence-based strategies for personal and organizational transformation. **Valuable for leaders, educators, and change agents** who want to understand the mechanics of sustainable change without massive resource investments.

## Literature and Human Connection

**On Earth We're Briefly Gorgeous** by Ocean Vuong (Penguin Press)
*Recommended by: McKinsey Global Publishing team*

This semi-autobiographical novel, written as a letter from son to illiterate mother, explores themes of trauma, identity, and family across generations. Vuong's poetic prose examines the immigrant experience and LGBTQ+ identity. **Perfect for readers seeking beautifully crafted literature that illuminates marginalized experiences**—especially meaningful for those interested in contemporary American narratives.

**Beloved** by Toni Morrison (Knopf)
*Recommended by: McKinsey Global Publishing team*

Morrison's Pulitzer Prize-winning masterpiece confronts the psychological trauma of slavery through the story of Sethe and her haunted past. The novel demonstrates how historical trauma reverberates through generations. **Essential reading for anyone seeking to understand American history and its ongoing impact**—particularly valuable for leaders working on diversity, equity, and inclusion initiatives.

**The Vegetarian** by Han Kang (Hogarth)
*Recommended by: McKinsey Global Publishing team*

This Nobel Prize-winning novel explores a woman's rebellion against societal expectations in South Korea through her refusal to eat meat. Kang examines gender roles, mental health, and individual agency within oppressive systems. **Compelling for readers interested in feminist literature and contemporary global fiction**—especially relevant given discussions about women's rights and social expectations.

## Science and Natural World

**The Light Eaters: How the Unseen World of Plant Intelligence Offers a New Understanding of Life on Earth** by Zoë Schlanger
*Recommended by: Kat Downs Mulder, Yahoo News*

Schlanger reveals groundbreaking research on plant consciousness and communication, challenging our understanding of intelligence in nature. The book explores how plants process information and respond to their environment. **Fascinating for science enthusiasts and anyone interested in expanding their conception of consciousness**—particularly engaging for those concerned about environmental and sustainability issues.

**Every Living Thing: The Great and Deadly Race to Know All Life** by Jason Roberts
*Recommended by: Yuval Atsmon, McKinsey Senior Partner*

Roberts chronicles the ambitious scientific effort to catalog and understand all life on Earth before species disappear. The book examines biodiversity, conservation challenges, and the race against extinction. **Essential for environmentally conscious readers and those interested in conservation biology**—especially relevant for business leaders considering sustainability strategies.

## Financial Wisdom and Economics

**The Psychology of Money: Timeless Lessons on Wealth, Greed, and Happiness** by Morgan Housel
*Recommended by: Maya Horgan Famodu, Ingressive Capital*

Housel explores how human psychology shapes financial decisions, revealing why smart people make poor money choices. The book emphasizes behavior over technical knowledge in building wealth. **Invaluable for anyone making financial decisions**—from individual investors to corporate executives managing budgets and investments.

**Our Dollar, Your Problem: An Insider's View of Seven Turbulent Decades of Global Finance, and the Road Ahead** by Kenneth Rogoff
*Recommended by: Glenn Hubbard, Columbia Business School*

Harvard economist Rogoff examines the dollar's role in global finance and its implications for international economics. He analyzes currency crises, monetary policy, and globalization's financial architecture. **Essential for finance professionals, policymakers, and business leaders** operating in global markets or making international investment decisions.

## Memoirs and Personal Transformation

**Educated** by Tara Westover (Random House)
*Recommended by: McKinsey Global Publishing team*

Westover's powerful memoir chronicles her journey from a survivalist family in Idaho to earning a Cambridge PhD. Her story explores education's transformative power and the cost of challenging family beliefs. **Compelling for anyone interested in education, family dynamics, and personal transformation**—particularly inspiring for those from unconventional backgrounds pursuing higher education.

**I Am Nobody's Slave: How Uncovering My Family's History Set Me Free** by Lee Hawkins (Amistad)
*Recommended by: McKinsey Global Publishing team*

Former Wall Street Journal reporter Hawkins traces his family's 400-year American journey, examining how slavery and Jim Crow affected successive generations. His research reveals how historical trauma shapes contemporary identity. **Essential for readers interested in family history, racial justice, and personal healing**—especially valuable for those exploring their own family narratives.

## Specialized Interests and Professional Development

**The Next Conversation: Argue Less, Talk More** by Jefferson Fisher (Tarcher)
*Recommended by: McKinsey Global Publishing team*

Communication expert Fisher provides practical strategies for improving difficult conversations and reducing conflict. The book offers frameworks for productive dialogue in personal and professional settings. **Valuable for managers, parents, and anyone seeking to improve their communication skills**—especially useful for those in leadership positions requiring frequent difficult conversations.

**101 Essays That Will Change the Way You Think** by Brianna Wiest
*Recommended by: Daniel Trujillo, McKinsey Ethics Officer*

This collection of transformative essays challenges conventional thinking patterns and offers new perspectives on personal growth. Wiest explores topics from mindfulness to career development. **Perfect for readers seeking personal development and new frameworks for thinking about life and work**—especially appealing to those interested in philosophy and self-improvement.

## Conclusion and Reading Strategy

McKinsey's 2025 recommendations reflect leaders' recognition that **exceptional performance requires both analytical rigor and human understanding**. The list balances practical leadership guidance with literature that expands emotional intelligence and cultural awareness.

For maximum value, consider reading across categories rather than within specialties. Pair a leadership book with a novel, or follow a historical analysis with a memoir. This cross-pollination approach mirrors how successful leaders integrate diverse perspectives to solve complex problems.

The emphasis on translated literature, marginalized voices, and interdisciplinary thinking suggests that tomorrow's leaders will need cultural fluency and cognitive flexibility as much as technical expertise. These books collectively argue that the future belongs to those who can synthesize insights across domains while remaining deeply human in their approach to leadership and life.
